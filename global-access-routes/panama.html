About the Canal

Connecting Two Oceans

The Panama Canal is one of the world's most strategically important waterways, enabling ships to pass between the Atlantic and Pacific without rounding Cape Horn. Opened in 1914 and expanded in 2016, it handles over 14,000 vessel transits annually.
